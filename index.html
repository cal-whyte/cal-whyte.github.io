About Me

I am currently a PhD student at Florida Tech working under the guidance of Dr. Luis Henry Quiroga-Nuñez as well as Dr. Manasvi Lingam. My research is focused on evaluating the habitability of exoplanets orbiting white dwarf stars. In 2021 I graduated from Lincoln Memorial University with a BS in Pre-Med Biology. I then transferred to Florida Tech where I earned a BS in Astrobiology in 2023 before transitioning into the Space Sciences PhD program starting that same year.

Research & Publications

My current research focuses on analyzing the habitable zones of white dwarf stars, and the unique challenges of sustaining life around a cooling primary star. White dwarfs are formed from stars like our Sun after they have exhausted their primary source of energy and expelled their outer layers, leaving behind an incredibly dense core that emits residual thermal energy and slowly cools. As a result of this cooling, the region around the white dwarf in which a planet can maintain a surface temperature suitable for life to exist will move inward as the star ages. This phenomenon is shown in the animation to the right where the orange and blue circles mark the minimum and maximum distance from the star that it is expected a planet could host life.
The main scientific goals of my research are to further constrain the boundaries of this habitable zone with more detailed modelling, improve our understanding of the necessary conditions required by a habitable planet in this zone, and contribute to observational programs that look for such planets and signs that they could host life. I am looking forward to exploring these fascinating systems deeper by modelling the impact that white dwarfs have on the atmospheric dynamics and chemistry of their planets.
